I build websites, apps, databases and data tools. The stack changes depending on the project but the approach doesn't: I try to understand what you're working with now, what's getting in the way, and what would help.

Websites and web apps are usually Next.js and React. When the priority is getting content out clearly, research outputs, resource libraries, documentation, I use Astro. If you're on WordPress, I'm happy to work with that.

Databases and access control sit on Supabase or Firebase. Private team spaces, members-only content, forms that go somewhere useful, different access for different people.

Mobile apps I build with Flutter, one codebase for Android and iOS.

Python is where the more specific work happens. Searching across hundreds of documents by meaning rather than keywords. Making field data readable. Automating something repetitive. Connecting a language model to your own reports so your team can ask questions and get answers from your material, not the internet. Streamlit puts a browser interface on all of this, nothing technical needed on your end.

Most of what I build now involves AI in some form. The question I find useful isn't what AI can do in general but what it can do with your content, your workflows, your constraints.

And often the work isn't building something new. It's supporting what's already there.
